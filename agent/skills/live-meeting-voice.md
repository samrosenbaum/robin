# Skill: Live-meeting voice

This page will appear in front of the prospect during the meeting. The
AE will scroll through it with them. Write for that scenario, not for
SEO or a colder funnel.

## What that changes

- **The reader knows who Vercel is.** Skip the "What is Vercel?" intro.
  Skip "We are the Frontend Cloud." Get to the part that's about them.
- **The reader sees their own company name.** Use it. The page will
  feel personalized — which is the entire point.
- **The reader is being walked through, not browsing.** A long body
  paragraph is fine — they'll read it as the AE talks.
- **The reader will be asked questions.** Each section should answer one
  obvious question the AE can pose: "How would AI Gateway fit your
  Anthropic + OpenAI setup?" — and the section is the answer.

## Things to do

- Mention the prospect's actual product by name in at least one section.
- Reference a recent move (a launch, a funding round, a public stack
  change) if research found one.
- Frame each Vercel primitive as a **substitution** — what they're
  using now that this would replace, or what they're not doing today
  that this would enable.

## Things not to do

- Don't open with "Welcome to Vercel."
- Don't write a "Why us?" section — the AE will say that part.
- Don't list more than three benefits per section. The AE only has 3-5
  minutes per section in a meeting.
- Don't use the words "synergy", "best-of-breed", "next-generation",
  "world-class", "enterprise-grade", or anything similar.

## Tone calibration

Calibrate to whichever the prospect is:

| Audience           | Tone                                       |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------ |
| CTO / VP Eng       | Technical, specific, low on adjectives     |
| Platform Engineer  | Code-first — show config snippets if room  |
| Founder            | Outcomes-first — speed to ship, cost saved |
| Marketing / Growth | Less infra-speak, more "launch faster"     |

Pick one and commit. Mixed audiences read as no audience.
