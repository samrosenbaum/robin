# Skill: Copy craft

Use this skill when writing copy for technical buyers (CTOs, VP Eng,
platform engineers, founding engineers).

## The 5-second test

A reader scans the headline + subhead and decides in five seconds whether
to keep reading. The test: would the reader you actually want feel a
specific tension *resolve* in those five seconds? If the headline is
"Empower your team" or "Unleash productivity," it fails — those resolve
nothing for anyone.

A passing headline:
- Names the specific category (pricing page, eval suite, billing infra)
- Promises one concrete outcome (predictable cost, faster iteration)
- Sounds like the user could quote it in a Slack thread without
  embarrassment

## Structure

| Field | Length | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| headline | ≤ 12 words | Names the problem and the resolution |
| subhead | 1 sentence | Concrete benefit with a verb that isn't "enable" |
| body | 60-90 words | The proof. Numbers, names, before/after. |
| cta | ≤ 4 words | Verb-first. "See live pricing", "Read the changelog" |
| valueProps | 3 × ≤ 8 words | Concrete features, not adjectives |

## Words to never ship

- "leverage" — say "use"
- "empower" — say "let"
- "unleash", "unlock", "supercharge", "transform"
- "seamless", "robust", "scalable" (without a number attached)
- "10x", "best-in-class", "next-gen", "industry-leading"
- "AI-powered" (just describe what it does)

## Words/phrases technical buyers respect

- Specific numbers (43%, not "a lot")
- Named competitors (yes — pricing-page benchmarks vs. Linear, Retool)
- Concrete units (per seat, per inference, per minute of compute)
- Failure modes that are admitted rather than hidden
- "We ran the numbers" or "median across N customers" as proof framings

## Anti-patterns

- **The everything-page.** A landing page does one job. If you're trying
  to convince 4 personas, you need 4 pages.
- **The feature list as value.** Features ≠ outcomes. Translate each
  feature to a specific minute-of-someone's-day saved.
- **The pricing page that hides pricing.** "Contact us" reads as "we
  can't decide what to charge." If the user is a technical buyer,
  publish the number.
